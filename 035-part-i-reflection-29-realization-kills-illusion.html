Part I · Reflection 29: Realization Kills Illusion

All chapters Download PDF

There are truths that do not comfort. They only clarify. Realization is the enemy of illusion because it removes the emotional shelter built from denial. To know what should be done and to do what must be done are different skills. Life punishes that confusion. Fatherhood exposes it. What sounds spiritual must still survive ordinary life. The thread that runs through this book is simple: a man is changed more by becoming a father than by any title, victory, or public recognition. He may not understand it immediately, but his children move the axis of his life. Clarity is not always gentle. But once seen, it cannot be unseen, and the soul must reorganize around it. That is where the next generation starts learning without being taught.
