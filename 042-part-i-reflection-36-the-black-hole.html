Part I · Reflection 36: The Black Hole

All chapters Download PDF

Pain has gravity. Separation proved that to me. There were mornings when my heart raced before thought, as if the body had already diagnosed the absence. If my pain had a shape, it would be a black hole: not only darkness, but a force dense enough to bend every thought around it. The danger of such pain is not only suffering; it is how easily suffering tries to become identity. In fatherhood, the abstract always becomes concrete. The thread that runs through this book is simple: a man is changed more by becoming a father than by any title, victory, or public recognition. He may not understand it immediately, but his children move the axis of his life. But gravity can also teach. It reveals what remains after illusions collapse. That is where becoming begins.
