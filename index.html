What this book is

Not advice. A reckoning.

The Becoming of a Father is a reflective work on fatherhood, responsibility, pain, transformation, and the moral architecture of love. It does not arrive with answers. It arrives with the questions a man asks himself at 3 a.m., the ones he usually keeps quiet.

It moves between intimate reflection, philosophical insight, and living legacy — part memoir, part private letter, part inheritance. Each chapter was written in the gap between who I was raised to be and who my children now need me to become.

From the author

Fortunat Bolangembe

I wrote this book the way most honest things get written — not on purpose, at first. It started as letters to my children, then notes to myself, then something I realized other fathers needed to read too. I'm not a therapist or a theologian. I'm a man who has tried, failed, and tried again to be worth the title.
