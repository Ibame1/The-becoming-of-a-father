Part I · Reflection 62: The Flower

All chapters Download PDF

of Life The Flower of Life became one of the clearest symbols for this book because it says something fatherhood also says: nothing stands alone. Every circle touches another. Every generation modifies the next. A father is not the whole design. He is a circle within it. But the integrity of his circle influences what touches it. Stability travels. So does damage. Children force philosophy to touch the ground. The thread that runs through this book is simple: a man is changed more by becoming a father than by any title, victory, or public recognition. He may not understand it immediately, but his children move the axis of his life. To think in generations is one of the first disciplines fatherhood demands. That is where a father is separated from performance.
